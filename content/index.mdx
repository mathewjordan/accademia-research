The Accademia di San Luca was founded in Rome in the late 16th century as an academy for painters, sculptors, and architects. Much about the origins of the Accademia—including its governance, finances, teaching, and membership—has largely remained hidden in notarial records. Archival document transcriptions and other research materials published to this website shed light on the breadth of the Accademia’s activities from around 1590 to 1657, including proceedings of meetings, financial and legal transactions, property rentals, and other records. 

## How to navigate this repository 

[**Resources**](/search)

This site includes three collections related to the Accademia di San Luca and to the city of Rome: 1) **notarial documents**, 2) **historic maps**, and 3) **guidebooks**. You can search any item in these collections by using the search bar at the top of each page. You can search by date, words (name of locations, artists, notaries, key terms), or enter, for instance, the word “map” to see all the maps. By clicking on Resources (top right), a grid of all the items will appear along with document’s transcriptions (Annotations tab). You can refine your research by using key terms, dates, place name, or other entries according to the metadata that you find in an item-dedicated page. 

[**Timeline**](/timeline)

A timeline tracing key moments of the foundation of the Accademia di San Luca, its building, and its first *principi*.

[**Research**](/research)

Additional research material includes five bilingual essays about the early religious buildings that hosted the Guild of Painters, and the Church of Santi Luca and Martina. 

[**About**](/about)

First launched as a research project at the **Center for Advanced Study in the Visual Arts** at the National Gallery of Art in 2007, *The Early History of the Accademia di San Luca* endeavors to make accessible archival documents concerning the origins of the Accademia. Supported by past grants from the Getty Foundation and the Samuel H. Kress Foundation and directed by Interim Dean Peter M. Lukehart, the research team has transcribed and published hundreds of early modern documents in Italian and Latin.

*Please note:* Transcriptions of notarial documents pertain to the Accademia di San Luca only. A document might contain writing that is not transcribed and it is not related to the Accademia.
