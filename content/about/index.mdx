This site is created with [Canopy IIIF](https://canopy-iiif.github.io/docs/), a static HTML generator designed for digital scholarship collections using the [IIIF](https://iiif.io/) APIs (generally pronounced “triple-eye-eff”). This website and [associated research publications](https://www.nga.gov/research/center/early-history-accademia-di-san-luca#a-section-header-p162966) are designed around sustainability, reusability, and minimal resources. The research team uses [Tropy](https://tropy.org/) to curate image collections and metadata, the [Tropiiify extension](https://github.com/arkalab/tropiiify) to export IIIF collections in three main content types (documents, maps, and guidebooks), and [GitHub Pages](https://docs.github.com/en/pages) to publish source files. These interoperable components are open source and free to use with relatively low initial cost. They can be deployed by researchers who wish to generate their own projects and questions from the same source materials. 

**New features launched with this version of the research project website include:**
 
- Enhanced search features and filters. 
- Annotated key terms in document transcriptions. 
- Reusable IIIF manifests and collections for all resources. 

## Digital project history 

The initial goal of The Early History of the Accademia di San Luca research project was to build a database using Extensible Markup Language (XML) and the international Text Encoding Initiative (TEI) guidelines to mark names, dates, and places in document transcriptions. By 2010, the project website was available to search and filter documents. In 2014, the rich TEI encoding created from the archival documents was no longer supported by the digital platforms employed by the National Gallery of art at the time, and the project team built a new system with Adobe CQ5 (later AEM), which also allowed for easier content updates. Starting in 2016, the revived site featured deep zoomable maps of sixteenth- and seventeenth-century Rome using the International Image Interoperability Framework (IIIF). Historical guidebooks describing the city and churches of Rome were added in 2021–2023. In 2023-2024, approximately 200 new documents augmented the original selection. 

In 2025-2026, during the rebuilding of the National Gallery of Art’s latest website, the project was migrated to its current home on GitHub (described above). 

## Archives and transcription conventions 

The Early History of the Accademia di San Luca brings together notarial records from the collections of documents pertaining to the offices of the Trenta Notai Capitolini (TNC) and the Tribunale Civile del Senato (TCS) held at the Archivio di Stato di Roma (ASR). Many of these documents concerning the institutional history of the Accademia di San Luca were previously thought to be lost. They include: 

- rental agreements and records of legal disputes regarding the collection of rent payments. 
- transactions with the workers who were charged with the renovation of the Accademia's original, derelict church. 
- inventories of the Accademia’s art and library collections. 
- evidence of the institution's increasing control over the production and appraisal of works of art. 
- details of the internal strife that marked the Accademia's first decades of existence. 
- evidence of the teaching of young artists in Rome. 
 
The transcriptions are organized by personal name, place name, key term, document type, notary name, and year. The documents are intended for study by researchers of early modern Italy, and to encourage their wider use towards identifying of additional archival material. 

## Bibliography and archived versions 

The research project also maintains a [Zotero database of bibliographical resources](https://www.zotero.org/groups/5626268/accademia_di_san_luca/library) on the history of the Accademia and its most prominent members. A web recorded version of the [previous project website (2015–2025)](https://wayback.archive-it.org/22902/20250331142215/https:/www.nga.gov/accademia/en/intro.html) is available at the Internet Archive. Certain features, like search and image zoom, will not work on the archived site.
